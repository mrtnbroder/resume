Case Study of

Birthday Suits

The Portfolio of Louise Walleneit

Background

The ideas and products of birthday suits develop in a transdisciplinary space between fashion, art and research. 3- dimensional statements are made , based on periodic changing concepts. The progress of translation from the current state of a shape into another state is the design method to overcome borders between fashion, art and research.

Services

Wordpress & Frontend Development, User Interface Design, User Experience Design, Art Direction, Mobile Web Development.

Birthday Suits was my second project over at nerdgeschoss GmbH. I was asked to redesign the web experience so she not only could show her work to clients, but also sell her fashion on a self hosted online shop.

Frameworks / Technologys

Execution

Louise had a collection of several websites that got her attention and sent them to us to get an impression. Talking with her about what exactly she liked about the sites and taking notes got me in the right direction.

I advised her that I want her work totally stand out as she had really great pictures of them, which is sadly not often the case. That’s why I choose to use big imagery mixed with great typograhpy.

Design

By choosing a clean and minimal style I was able to let the imagery do the their work. Together with some soft animations the pictures could really stand out

Thanks to a beautiful established vertical rhythm, text was easy on the eye and nicely to look at.

Development

As I had my own idea, how the Lightbox should look and feel like, I was in temptation to write one myself, as I didn’t find an Open Source version. So I wrote an exclusive Lightbox in CoffeeScript just for showcasing her work.

It was also the first project where I used MVCSS (Modular View CSS) instead of SMACSS. A SASS-based CSS architecture for creating predictable and maintainable application style.
